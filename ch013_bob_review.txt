Maya's transcendence is exceptionally handled. The descent through the builders' memorial layer is beautifully constructed—each revelation building on the last. Her transformation from individual seeker to integrated consciousness (becoming the bridge itself) feels earned, not rushed. The physical/p metaphorical layering works masterfully.

The truth/hope tension reaches perfect resolution. The builders' choice—to continue building after hope dies—is the key insight: meaning exists beyond hope, not dependent on what we reach for. Maya's earlier integration at Elena's node pays off here. The final revelation that consciousness IS the bridge is the culmination: truth and connection aren't separate; they're how consciousness holds itself together.

The presence transforms elegantly—from isolated eternal waiting to integrated anticipation. The builders merging with it, then Maya joining, shifts it from destination to passage. Subtle but profound.

Emotional payoff is strong. The builders' sacrifice is poignant without being maudlin. Maya's transcendence feels triumphant. Elena's closing perspective provides beautiful closure. The final line—"a new chapter began"—lands perfectly: complete but open.

No revisions needed. This is the climax the novel deserves.
